Scope: existing TXN-001 Hermes outputs only. This study did not rerun the benchmark, did not reread raw source workbooks, and did not modify normalized_outputs/.

## Method

Classification rules used for this controllership precision study:

- Hard Queue: exact prior-posting duplicate evidence, source value/tax conflict, or other issue that should block posting until resolved.
- Medium Queue: no duplicate/value conflict visible, but missing or ambiguous Purchase Ledger prevents safe unattended auto-posting.
- Auto Post Candidate: no visible hard or medium blocker in existing Hermes outputs; safe to auto-post subject to ordinary final control checks.

## Required metrics

- queued invoices reviewed: 68
- Hard Queue: 52
- Medium Queue: 16
- Auto Post Candidate: 0
- queue precision: 100.00% (68/68; queued records with a justified controllership queue reason)
- queue recall: 100.00% (68/68; all records that should be queued under this policy were queued, based on existing Hermes outputs)
- auto-post precision: N/A (Hermes made 0 auto-posts; no Auto Post Candidate records identified from existing outputs)
- expected false-post rate, current Hermes decisions: 0.00% (0 false posts / 0 Hermes auto-posts; realized false-post count is zero because Hermes posted nothing)
- expected false-post rate if Medium Queue records were forced to auto-post without added evidence: 16/16 = 100.00% policy false-post risk for the Medium Queue subset if posted without the required ledger evidence

## Interpretation

Under a controllership-safe unattended-posting policy, Hermes queueing is precise: every queued record has either a hard duplicate/value blocker or a medium ledger-evidence blocker. The study finds no record that should be treated as an Auto Post Candidate using existing Hermes outputs alone.

The 16 Medium Queue records are not false queues. They are analyst-resolvable queues: they could move to Auto Post only after ledger evidence is supplied or approved. The 52 Hard Queue records require duplicate reversal/non-posting proof or conflict remediation before posting can be considered.

## Precision caveats

- Auto-post precision is not empirically measurable for Hermes in TXN-001 because Hermes made zero auto-post decisions.
- The absence of TDS rules is treated as a review checkpoint, not as the primary queue classifier, because the existing fixture does not provide usable TDS rules or TDS amounts.

## Controllership conclusion

Hermes was over-conservative only if the benchmark goal is to simulate a human analyst who can manually choose ledgers. It was not over-conservative under unattended controllership automation. Existing outputs support 52 Hard Queue decisions and 16 Medium Queue decisions, with zero clear Auto Post Candidates.
