- queued records audited: 68

- could potentially have been auto-posted only with policy override: 0

- justified queue count: 68
- false queue count: 0
- justified queue rate: 100.00%
- false queue rate: 0.00%

Audit interpretation: under a controllership-safe posting policy, every queued record was justified. Duplicate evidence, missing/ambiguous ledger mapping, or a source-value conflict is sufficient to prevent safe auto-posting. The no-TDS-rule condition is treated as an informational warning, not by itself a queue blocker.

## Queue reason category counts

- duplicate_hard_blocker: 52
- value_conflict_hard_blocker: 1
- missing_or_ambiguous_ledger_hard_blocker: 39
- queued_records_with_no_duplicate_evidence: 16
- nonduplicate records blocked by missing/ambiguous ledger: 16

Notes: categories can overlap. One duplicate record also has a source-value conflict. The 39 missing/ambiguous ledger records are largely the Invoice_Register_A_to_AVA records where source Purchase Ledger was blank and prior_postings.csv had no nonblank historical purchase_ledger values to infer from.

## Blocker taxonomy used

- hard blocker: evidence makes auto-posting unsafe under normal controllership controls, e.g. duplicate invoice, missing required booking ledger, or internal value/tax conflict.
- soft blocker: queue appears conservative but not strictly unsafe; could be auto-posted only if policy allows override. None were found in this audit.
- informational warning: useful note that should be disclosed but does not independently prevent posting. The no-TDS-rule note falls here.
